# Mobile Pilot Farmer Feedback Protocol

Use this protocol for the first 3-5 farmer pilot sessions.

## Pilot Hypothesis

Farmers may be more willing to capture useful farm information when they can speak a quick note and attach photos at the moment work happens, instead of stopping to fill out structured forms.

## Observation Prompts

- Did the farmer understand what to do first?
- Could the farmer record a voice note without help?
- Did the farmer add photos naturally?
- Did farm-place setup match how the farmer thinks about the farm?
- Did the timeline help the farmer find what they captured?
- Did recovery-copy wording feel understandable and trustworthy?
- Did any screen imply upload, sync, AI, or backup in a confusing way?

## Interview Questions

1. What did you want to record first?
2. Was speaking a note easier than typing a record?
3. When would photos be useful?
4. What did you expect the app to do with your voice note?
5. What would make you trust the recovery copy?
6. What felt slow, confusing, or unnecessary?
7. Would you use this for one week of real farm work? Why or why not?

## Success Criteria

- At least 3 farmers can record and review a voice/photo note with minimal help.
- Farmers understand that data stays on the device unless they export it.
- Farmers can explain the difference between a saved note and a recovery package.
- At least 2 farmers identify real situations where quick capture would replace a missed note or memory.

## Stop Criteria

Pause pilot expansion if:

- notes or photos do not persist after relaunch;
- recovery package creation fails on test devices;
- farmers misunderstand the app as cloud-backed or AI-interpreted;
- install or permission issues block normal first use.
